Classical antiquity, also known as the classical era, classical period, classical age, or simply antiquity, is the period of cultural European history between the 8th century and the 5th century comprising the interwoven civilizations of Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome. It encompassed Greco-Roman culture, which played a major role in the Mediterranean sphere of influence and in the creation of Western civilization, shaping areas as diverse as law, architecture, art, language, poetry, rhetoric, politics, and philosophy.
